Let's Brew

  • French Press
  • Pour-over
  • Cold Brew
  • Vietnam Drip
  • Coffee Brewer

French Press

The French Press has another 'thing' that can make your coffee tastier if you use it properly. Without a paper filter, the French press produces a richer, softer, stronger aroma and certainly a pleasure.

Pour-over

water that is sprinkled over the coffee grounds flows into a serving glass. makes the coffee taste smoother and clearer without any crema on the surface.

Cold Brew

This process will produce a unique taste, sweet and low acidity. Even without sugar or other sweeteners, Cold Brew Coffee tastes sweeter and softer.

Vietnam Drip

The result of extraction through the dripper which is shaped like a metal cup produces coffee with a taste that tends to be sour but mixed with the sweetness of creamer.

Coffee Brewer

Drip coffee is a convenient way to make great coffee. With the right grind and pure water, you can brew a fantastic cup of coffee in a coffee brewer.

Beans

Our Coffee Beans

Arabika

In addition to the distinctive taste of Arabica coffee in the form of a light viscosity (body) and a higher acidity level than other coffees, Arabica coffee also has a very unique aroma profile, some of which have a distinctive aroma resembling lemon (lemony). resembling honey (honeyed) and even a distinctive aroma resembling chocolate (chocolaty). The distinctive aroma of the brewed product can indeed provide added value to the resulting Arabica coffee flavored product.

Robusta

Robusta has a taste that tends to be strong and bitter. The taste that is owned by Robusta is actually liked by many coffee lovers who are more bitter. What's more, the bitter taste actually also indicates a high caffeine content in Robusta, which is 1.5-3.3%, or 2 times the caffeine content of Arabica.

Aceh Gayo

The unique taste makes Aceh Gayo Coffee win the hearts of coffee connoisseurs. A strong aroma with a slightly bitter taste accompanied by a low level of acidity is the hallmark of this coffee.
